PROJECT SYSTEMS

Working systems

Hampden County Mesh currently operates a MeshCore Room Observer, a Linux-based MeshCore-Hub, and local tools for logging, testing, and documenting network activity.

These systems support the project, but they are not the whole network. A useful county-wide mesh depends on more local nodes, better coverage notes, reliable documentation, and people learning how the system works.

Backend services

Backend services may include logging tools, local broker services, scripts, dashboards, and future data publishing. These are important, but they are mostly behind-the-scenes infrastructure.

For most visitors, the important questions are simpler: what is online, where coverage is being tested, how to join, and how to help build practical local communications.

STATUS DEFINITIONS

Online

A system exists, is documented, and is currently considered usable for project work.

Development

A feature or service is being built, tested, or planned, but should not be treated as complete or reliable yet.

Building

The project is actively expanding through field work, documentation, equipment testing, and community participation.

Unknown

A system or coverage area has not been recently verified. Unknown does not always mean offline; it means more testing is needed.

IMPORTANT LIMITS

Hampden County Mesh is a community communications and learning project. It is not an emergency service, not a replacement for 911, and not a guarantee of coverage during an outage.

The goal is to build useful local options: practical skills, shared documentation, field-tested coverage knowledge, and communications tools that can help people coordinate when ordinary systems are unavailable, overloaded, or inconvenient.

The network will become more useful as more people test, document, host, teach, and maintain pieces of it.
