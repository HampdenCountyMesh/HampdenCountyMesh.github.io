Hampden County Mesh is a community effort focused on resilient communications, radio experimentation, mesh networking, and local infrastructure development throughout Hampden County and Western Massachusetts.

OUR MISSION

Hampden County Mesh exists to encourage experimentation, education, and community involvement in decentralized communications technologies.

We believe communities benefit from understanding and building their own communications infrastructure, whether for learning, preparedness, public service, or technical curiosity.

The project welcomes newcomers and experienced operators alike. No prior radio or networking experience is required.

WHAT WE WORK WITH

MeshCore

LoRa-based mesh networking focused on lightweight, resilient communications and infrastructure development.

Meshtastic

Open-source mesh networking with mapping, GPS integration, and mobile applications.

Radio Communications

Amateur Radio, GMRS, FRS, MURS, CB, and other communications technologies.

Infrastructure

Gateways, observers, repeaters, antennas, servers, mapping systems, and community resources.

CURRENT PROJECT STATUS

The project is currently focused on building foundational infrastructure and establishing a local network presence.

Current systems include an operational Room Observer, a Linux-based Meshcore-Hub, MQTT services, and ongoing network monitoring efforts.

Additional infrastructure, mapping tools, and community resources are planned.
