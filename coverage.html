WHY LOCAL TESTING MATTERS
Field testing helps turn individual experiments into shared local knowledge.

Coverage is shaped by real terrain.

Hampden County has hills, valleys, wooded areas, dense neighborhoods, old brick buildings, river corridors, highways, campuses, hospitals, parks, and rural edges. Radio coverage changes across all of that.

A device that works from one hilltop may not work from a nearby low spot. A node near a window may behave differently than the same node inside a building or vehicle.

Field testing helps the project learn what actually works here, not just what should work on paper.

REPORT COVERAGE

Coverage reports do not need to be perfect. A short, honest field note is useful if it tells other people what was tested and what happened.

Useful reports include the date, general location, device, antenna, placement, service or mode tested, what was heard, what was not heard, and any notes about terrain, buildings, trees, or weather.

You do not need to publish exact home addresses or private locations. General areas, public landmarks, road corridors, parks, neighborhoods, and town-level notes are often enough.

COVERAGE PRINCIPLES

Use real places

Test from places people actually use: homes, streets, parks, workplaces, hilltops, libraries, campuses, vehicles, and community spaces.

Write down failures

Dead spots and failed tests are useful. Knowing where something does not work helps the project plan better nodes and repeaters.

Protect privacy

General areas are usually enough. Do not expose private addresses, private property, or someone else’s exact location without permission.

Repeat tests

Coverage changes with antenna placement, firmware, weather, power, foliage, and new infrastructure. Repeated tests matter.

MAP STATUS

A public Hampden County coverage map is a project goal, but the map should be based on real notes and honest status. Until then, the map is best treated as a planning tool, not a live network display.

Future map work may include public coverage areas, generalized node reports, field test points, observer notes, and live data where it is accurate and appropriate to publish.

The goal is useful public information without exposing private homes, private infrastructure, or unfinished backend details that are not helpful to visitors.
