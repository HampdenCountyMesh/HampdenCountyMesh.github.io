Observed Activity Work

Public activity tools for this site are still in development. The goal is to eventually show useful, public-safe observed activity without turning the map into a claim that every visible node belongs to Hampden County Mesh.

A dedicated map address is reserved at map.hampdencountymesh.org. For now, it points here while future live or near-live observed activity tools are prepared.

Normal users do not need site-maintained infrastructure to get started. A basic node, the right settings, and a reasonable location are enough to begin testing.

Local Terrain
Small radio devices behave differently depending on terrain, buildings, antennas, and height.

Radio behaves differently from place to place.

Hampden County has hills, valleys, wooded areas, dense neighborhoods, old brick buildings, river corridors, highways, campuses, hospitals, parks, and rural edges. All of that affects small radios.

A device near a window may behave differently than the same device deeper inside a building or vehicle. A little more height, a better antenna, or a different location can make a real difference.
