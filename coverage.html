Portable MeshCore field testing in Hampden County.

Coverage starts with real-world testing.

Mesh activity maps are useful, but local testing is what shows how devices actually behave across terrain, neighborhoods, roads, hills, buildings, and outdoor operating locations.

Hampden County Mesh uses portable nodes, fixed infrastructure, observer logs, and community reports to build a clearer picture of where MeshCore traffic can be heard and how the network performs.

Public map points may be generalized or omitted for privacy and safety. Observed traffic and field testing are documented separately from guaranteed coverage.

CURRENT INFRASTRUCTURE

Hampden Room Observer

Indoor Heltec V3 observer used for MeshCore monitoring, logging, MQTT testing, and future status reporting.

MeshCore-Hub

Linux-based infrastructure host for local logging, MQTT services, documentation, and future automation.

HampdenCountyTreeNode

SEEED SenseCAP Solar Node P1-Pro deployed as fixed outdoor infrastructure supporting coverage expansion and field testing.

Cassiopeia

SEEED Wio Tracker L1 Pro companion node used for portable testing and observer ownership/configuration.

KNOWN COVERAGE

Coverage is currently strongest near active infrastructure, participating operators, and areas where field testing has been performed.

The public map is not yet a guarantee of real-time network availability. It is a foundation for documenting infrastructure and improving local coverage over time.

REGIONAL ALIGNMENT

Hampden County Mesh is focused on local community building while aligning with the existing BDL / Windsor Locks regional mapping area where appropriate.

The goal is not to duplicate nearby communities, but to document Hampden County activity clearly and make local participation easier for new operators.

PLANNED EXPANSION
