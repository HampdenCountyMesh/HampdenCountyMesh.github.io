Contributing Notes

Hampden County Mesh is more useful when people share what they are learning locally. This page explains what kinds of notes are helpful, how to share them, and what details should stay private.

Public notes should help people learn without exposing private homes, exact coordinates, keys, passwords, Wi-Fi details, private messages, or anything someone did not agree to share.
What Helps Signal Notes Node Notes Photos Privacy Ways to Share Checklist

What kind of notes help

Useful notes do not need to be formal. A short, clear observation is often enough. The best notes explain what was tested, where it was tested in general terms, what equipment was used, and what happened.

Signal checks

Notes about what a device could hear, who heard a test message, and what changed when the device moved.

Node notes

General information about a portable node, fixed node, repeater, observer, antenna, or temporary setup.

Local terrain notes

Observations about hills, valleys, parks, buildings, road corridors, wooded areas, or other local conditions.

Guide corrections

Fixes for unclear wording, broken links, outdated information, typos, missing beginner context, or confusing instructions.

Photos

Useful public photos of devices, field checks, antennas, public places, or general Hampden County radio scenery.

Session ideas

Suggestions for places where people could meet, ask questions, test nodes, or learn mesh and radio basics.

Signal notes

Signal notes are some of the most useful contributions because they help people understand what works locally. A failed test can still be useful if it is described clearly.

Private details removed before sharing: yes / no
Notes:

Good examples

  • “Portable MeshCore node heard activity near Mittineague Park while outside, but not from inside the vehicle.”
  • “Window placement in Westfield worked better than desk placement.”
  • “Could hear activity from higher ground, but not from lower wooded areas nearby.”
  • “No replies during this check, but the device did hear a nearby observer.”

Less useful examples

  • “Nothing works.”
  • “Coverage is bad.”
  • “This device is the best.”
  • “The map showed something so it must work here.”

Specific, limited notes are easier to use than broad claims.

Photos

Photos can make the site and guides more useful, but they should be handled carefully. A good photo shows something useful without accidentally revealing private information.

Useful photo ideas

  • A node during a public field check
  • A device near a public park, trail, or overlook
  • Antenna placement examples
  • Public scenery that helps show Hampden County terrain
  • Workshop, table, or demo setups with permission
  • Before-and-after examples of safer or cleaner installs

Check before sharing photos

  • Does the photo show a private address?
  • Does it show a license plate, mail, screen, reflection, or badge?
  • Does it reveal exact private node placement?
  • Are people visible, and did they agree to be included?
  • Does the photo contain GPS or EXIF location data?
  • Does the photo show passwords, keys, QR codes, or private messages?

When in doubt, crop the photo, blur private details, remove metadata, or keep it private.

Guide edits and corrections

Guide improvements are useful, especially when they make the site clearer for beginners. Good edits do not need to be large.

Helpful edits

  • Fixing typos
  • Fixing broken links
  • Replacing confusing wording
  • Adding missing beginner context
  • Updating outdated device, link, or site references
  • Pointing to official documentation instead of copying long setup instructions
  • Making privacy warnings clearer

What to avoid

  • Adding private credentials, keys, or exact private locations
  • Copying long sections from other websites
  • Presenting guesses as confirmed local results
  • Turning beginner pages into dense technical manuals
  • Publishing someone else’s setup details without permission

Privacy and sensitive information

Hampden County Mesh should be useful without making people less safe or less comfortable participating. Public notes should be broad enough to help, but careful enough to avoid exposing private details.

Usually safe to share

  • Town or general area
  • Public landmark, park, hill, or road corridor
  • General terrain description
  • Device role
  • General antenna type or placement
  • Whether a setup is indoor, outdoor, mobile, fixed, or temporary
  • General results such as heard, not heard, weak, or reliable

Do not post publicly

  • Private keys
  • Passwords
  • Broker credentials
  • Wi-Fi names or Wi-Fi passwords
  • Private IP addresses
  • Exact home addresses
  • Exact private node coordinates without permission
  • Private messages or screenshots that reveal private details
  • Photos that reveal private locations unintentionally
  • Someone else’s name, contact info, or setup details without permission

Sensitive security issues should not be posted in a public channel. Use the security contact instead:

security@hampdencountymesh.org

Before sharing checklist

  • Did you remove private keys, passwords, and credentials?
  • Did you avoid exact private home locations?
  • Did you remove EXIF or location metadata from photos?
  • Did you avoid posting someone else’s details without permission?
  • Did you describe the general area clearly enough to be useful?
  • Did you say whether the result came from radio, an observer, MQTT, or a map?
  • Did you avoid presenting a guess as a confirmed result?
  • Did you avoid claiming guaranteed coverage or emergency service capability?

A careful note is better than a detailed note that exposes too much.
