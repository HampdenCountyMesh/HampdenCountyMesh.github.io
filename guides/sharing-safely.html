Do not post passwords, private keys, access tokens, broker credentials, private messages, or exact private locations without permission.
Safe to Share Check First Locations Maps Security

Usually safe to share

Useful technical context does not need to include every detail about a person or location.

Area

General location

A town, public place, road corridor, hill, or broad area is often enough to explain local results.

Device

Setup details

Device model, firmware version, antenna type, power source, and general placement are normally useful troubleshooting information.

Results

Short observations

Notes such as “heard activity near downtown” or “window placement worked better” can help other local users.

Docs

Public project information

Broken links, confusing instructions, public tool problems, and guide corrections are appropriate for Discord or GitHub.

Check before posting

Screenshots and photos often include more than the thing you intended to show.

Screenshots

  • Crop the image to the part that matters.
  • Remove passwords, keys, tokens, and credentials.
  • Check private messages and contact names.
  • Check browser tabs, bookmarks, notifications, and account details.
  • Remove exact private coordinates when they are not relevant.

Photos

  • Check addresses, license plates, screens, badges, and reflections.
  • Think about whether the photo reveals a private node location.
  • Check whether people in the photo are comfortable being posted.
  • Use a closer photo of the equipment when the surrounding location is not important.

Setup details

  • Do not post passwords, private keys, access tokens, or broker credentials.
  • Do not post private messages or someone else's information without permission.
  • Be careful with admin interfaces, internal infrastructure details, and anything that could provide access to a system.

Location sharing

The right amount of location detail depends on what you are describing. A public event can be named precisely. A private home installation usually does not need to be.

A town, neighborhood, public landmark, or general area is often enough for troubleshooting and coverage discussion.

General area

“Monson area,” “near downtown Springfield,” or “Southwick” gives useful regional context.

Public location

A park, trail, event site, or other clearly public location can usually be identified directly.

Private installation

Avoid adding exact coordinates, addresses, or detailed directions unless the location is intentionally public and the owner has agreed.

Node names

A useful node name helps people recognize what they are seeing without pointing directly at a private address.

  • Use a short and recognizable name.
  • A town, area, hill, park, or general landmark may be useful.
  • Add a role such as repeater, room, or observer when it helps.
  • Avoid addresses, apartment numbers, phone numbers, or other unnecessary personal details.

Maps and observed activity

The Hampden County Mesh map and analyzer display activity heard through participating observer systems.

A visible node or packet does not mean Hampden County Mesh owns that device, knows who operates it, or can confirm that your own radio can reach it directly.

Public map data also does not make additional private information fair game. Be careful about combining a public marker with names, addresses, photos, or other details that identify a person or private installation.

Where to share

Discord

Questions and discussion

Device help, setup questions, local observations, and normal project discussion.

GitHub

Website and documentation

Broken links, guide corrections, and public website or repository issues.

Email

Direct contact

Outreach, events, private questions, or information that should not begin in a public channel.

Security issues

Do not open a public Discord or GitHub discussion with exposed credentials, private keys, access tokens, or details that could help someone access or disrupt a system.

Send security concerns directly to:

security@hampdencountymesh.org

One last check

Before posting, ask whether the extra details in the image or message actually help explain what you are sharing. If they do not, crop them out or leave them out.
