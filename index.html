LOCAL MESH NETWORKING

Local mesh networking in Hampden County

Mesh radio is still new to a lot of people. This site is here to make it easier to get started, compare notes, and find other people nearby who are using MeshCore, Meshtastic, or other off-grid messaging tools. It is also a place to ask about node setup, antennas, repeaters, observers, and what works around Hampden County.

Most of this comes down to practical questions: what hardware people are using, where signals are getting through, what helps a node work better, and what people nearby have already learned.

HAMPDEN COUNTY TERRAIN MATTERS
Mount Tom radio towers overlooking Hampden County.

Local terrain makes radio interesting here.

Hampden County has hills, valleys, dense neighborhoods, wooded areas, old mill buildings, open farmland, and high spots like Mount Tom. All of that changes what a small LoRa radio can hear.

Sometimes moving a node across the room matters. Sometimes getting it near a window helps. Sometimes height matters more than power. The only real way to learn the local radio picture is to try things, compare notes, and see what works for other people nearby.
