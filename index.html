LOCAL MESH NETWORKING

MeshCore first, Meshtastic close behind.

Hampden County Mesh is centered on local MeshCore use: learning the hardware, comparing node locations, understanding repeaters and observers, and helping people get on the air.

Meshtastic is also part of the community. Many people come to mesh radio through Meshtastic first, and the same local terrain, antenna, battery, and placement lessons still matter.

Radio basics tie it together. FRS, GMRS, amateur radio, CB, and MURS are not the main focus here, but they are useful background for anyone learning how local communications work.

HAMPDEN COUNTY TERRAIN MATTERS
Mount Tom radio towers overlooking Hampden County.

Local terrain makes radio interesting here.

Hills, valleys, buildings, tree cover, antenna height, and device placement all affect what a node can hear. A setup that works well in one part of Hampden County may behave very differently a few towns away.

The point is not to make one official network map and call it done. The useful part is learning what works locally, sharing what people find, and making it easier for newcomers to choose a first setup.

Coverage testing still matters, but it belongs in the background: a practical tool for learning and improving the local mesh, not the whole identity of the site.
