Local radio, built around our region.

Mesh radios can pass short messages directly between nearby devices. When other nodes are available, messages may travel farther across the network without depending entirely on cell service or normal internet access.

These systems can be useful for everyday communication, field testing, community events, and added resilience during outages or disruptions. Hampden County Mesh is a place to learn what works across the hills, valleys, cities, and towns of Western Massachusetts.

New to mesh radio? Learn what you need and how to get started locally. Already have a device? Apply the recommended settings used around the region. Looking for local activity? Open the live map and explore recently observed radio traffic. Interested in better coverage? Learn how terrain, height, placement, and fixed nodes affect radio paths.

Explore the local mesh.

HCM provides public tools for viewing MeshCore activity observed through participating systems. The map can show recently seen nodes and packet paths, while the analyzer provides a closer look at received traffic.

These tools show observed activity, not a complete inventory of every node operating in the region.
